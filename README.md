# How Couples Met — Trends and Insights  

## Overview  
This project explores **how couples have met over time**, analyzing real-world survey data to understand the evolution of romantic connections.  
It visualizes how relationship formation has shifted from traditional meeting methods — such as **friends, family, or workplaces** — to **modern digital platforms**, uncovering how technology and changing social norms shape love and companionship today.  

---

## Objectives  
- Examine **how couples met** across different decades and demographic groups.  
- Analyze the **influence of technology** on modern relationships.  
- Visualize trends showing the **decline of traditional meeting places** and the **rise of online dating**.  
- Apply data visualization techniques to communicate **social and cultural changes** effectively.  

---

## Dataset  
The dataset includes survey responses on how couples first met, categorized by variables such as **year**, **gender**, and **relationship type**.  

| Column Name | Description |
|--------------|-------------|
| `met_year` | The year the couple first met |
| `how_met` | Method of meeting (e.g., friends, online, work, family) |
| `gender` | Gender of the respondent |
| `relationship_status` | Whether the relationship is ongoing or ended |
| `duration` | Duration of the relationship (in years) |

The dataset provides insight into **decade-wise changes** in social behavior and the growing role of **digital interaction** in relationships.  

---

## Key Steps and Analysis  

### 1. Data Loading and Cleaning  
- Imported the dataset using **pandas**.  
- Checked for missing values and normalized meeting method labels.  
- Filtered relevant features for trend visualization.  

### 2. Exploratory Analysis  
- Analyzed the distribution of how couples met across different **years**.  
- Grouped and aggregated data to identify patterns in **meeting trends**.  
- Explored relationships between **gender** and **meeting method**.  

### 3. Visualization  
- Created **bar plots** and **line charts** to visualize meeting trends.  
- Used **Seaborn** and **Matplotlib** for clear, publication-ready plots.  
- Highlighted key shifts, such as the **rise of online dating** since the 2000s.  

### 4. Interpretation  
- **Friends and family introductions** dominated before the 1990s.  
- **Workplace and school connections** were common in mid-decade relationships.  
- **Online platforms and apps** surged after the 2000s, marking a digital transformation in relationship dynamics.  

---

## Key Insights  
- The data shows a **cultural evolution** from community-based introductions to self-directed, online connections.  
- **Technology has diversified** the ways people meet, expanding opportunities but also changing expectations.  
- The shift reflects broader **social independence** and **digital adoption** patterns across generations.
